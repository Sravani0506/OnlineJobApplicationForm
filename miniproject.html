Our India

India is a rapidly growing global power, projected to become the world's third-largest economy by 2035 with a population of ~1.5 billion. As the largest democracy, it is a significant, ancient civilization with a 100,000-year history of non-aggression, now acting as a major hub for technology, culture, and economic development. Before & After India

Before 1947, India was a colonized, fragmented, and impoverished region under British rule, characterized by economic exploitation and lack of fundamental rights. After Independence, India became a democratic, secular republic that faced massive partition violence, refugee crises, and the crucial integration of 565 princely states.

Freedom Fighters

Indian freedom fighter theory centers on achieving complete liberation from foreign rule through diverse approaches, ranging from non-violent civil disobedience (Gandhian Satyagraha) to armed resistance and radical revolution. It emphasized self-sacrifice, "swaraj" (self-rule), national sovereignty, and the moral obligation to fight against oppression

Politician Leaders

Political leader theories examine how individuals gain power, influence society, and make decisions through various styles—including transformational, transactional, and situational approaches. These frameworks emphasize that effective leadership involves navigating both political cunning (winning power) and conscience (morality). Key theories range from innate traits to adaptive behavioral styles

Sports Persons

The psychology of sportspersons, often studied within sports psychology, focuses on the unique mental traits, motivations, and behaviors that differentiate athletes from non-athletes and influence their performance. Research suggests that elite athletes tend to be more extroverted, emotionally stable, and conscientious than non-athletes.
